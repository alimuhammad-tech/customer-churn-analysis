Business Insights
Executive Summary

This project analyzes customer churn patterns using customer demographics, contract information, internet services, payment methods, customer satisfaction, and customer lifetime value (CLTV). The objective is to identify the major factors contributing to customer churn and provide actionable recommendations to improve customer retention.

Key Insights
1. Overall Churn Rate

Approximately 26.54% of customers have churned, while nearly 67% remain active. This indicates that although the majority of customers stay with the company, a significant portion is still being lost.

2. Contract Type Influences Churn

Customers with Month-to-Month contracts experience substantially higher churn compared to customers with One-Year and Two-Year contracts. Longer contract commitments appear to improve customer retention.

3. Competitors Are the Primary Cause of Churn

The largest churn category is Competitor, suggesting that many customers leave because competing companies provide better offers, pricing, or services.

4. Better Offers Drive Customer Loss

Among all churn reasons, customers most frequently reported:

Competitor had better devices
Competitor offered better deals
Competitor offered more data

This indicates that pricing and promotional strategies significantly affect customer retention.

5. Internet Type Impacts Customer Retention

Customers using Fiber Optic Internet represent a larger portion of churned customers compared to other internet types, suggesting an opportunity to investigate pricing, service quality, or customer expectations.

6. Customer Satisfaction Is Strongly Related to Churn

Customers with lower satisfaction scores are considerably more likely to churn, while customers with higher satisfaction scores generally remain with the company.

7. Customer Lifetime Value

Customers who remain with the company have the highest average Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV), demonstrating the financial importance of retaining long-term customers.

8. Payment Method Distribution

Bank Withdrawal is the most commonly used payment method. Understanding whether certain payment methods correlate with higher churn can help design targeted retention strategies.

9. Revenue Impact

Although churned customers contribute to overall revenue, retained customers generate the largest share of long-term revenue, reinforcing the value of retention initiatives.

Conclusion

The analysis indicates that contract type, competitor offerings, customer satisfaction, and internet service characteristics are among the most influential factors affecting customer churn. Implementing targeted retention strategies based on these findings can improve customer loyalty, reduce churn, and increase long-term revenue.
